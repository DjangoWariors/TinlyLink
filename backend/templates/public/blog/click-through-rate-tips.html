Click-through rate is one of the most important metrics in digital marketing. Whether you're sharing links on social media, in emails, or through ads, these 10 proven strategies will help you get more clicks.

1. Use Branded Short Links

Generic short URLs (like kit.ly/x7k9f) look suspicious to many users. Branded short links (like brand.co/sale) build trust and can increase CTR by up to 34%. With TinlyLink, you can use your own custom domain for short links that reinforce your brand.

Generic link Lower trust
https://tly.co/x7k9f2m
Branded link Higher trust
https://brand.co/summer-sale

2. Write Compelling Link Text

The text surrounding your link matters as much as the link itself. Use action-oriented language that creates urgency or curiosity. Instead of "click here," try "See the results" or "Grab your spot before they are gone."

3. Optimize Your Custom Slugs

When you use custom slugs (the text after the domain), make them descriptive and relevant. A slug like /pricing-guide tells users what to expect, while /abc123 tells them nothing.

tinlylink.com/a8x2k
tinlylink.com/free-trial

4. A/B Test Your Links

Create two versions of the same link with different UTM content tags and test them against each other. Try different placements (header vs. footer), different CTAs ("Learn more" vs. "Get started"), or different visual treatments. Let the data decide.

5. Place Links Above the Fold

In emails and on web pages, links placed above the fold (visible without scrolling) consistently outperform those buried at the bottom. Your primary CTA link should be one of the first things users see.

6. Time Your Posts Strategically

Analyze your link analytics to find when your audience is most active. Post times vary by platform and audience, but you can use click-over-time data from your TinlyLink dashboard to identify your specific peak engagement windows.

7. Add Visual CTAs

Buttons outperform text links in almost every context. In emails, a well-designed button CTA can increase clicks by 28% compared to a plain text link. Use contrasting colors that stand out from the surrounding content.

8. Create Urgency

Time-limited offers and link expiration dates create a sense of urgency that drives clicks. TinlyLink lets you set automatic expiration dates on links, and you can communicate this in your messaging: "Link expires Friday" or "Limited availability."

9. Use QR Codes for Physical Touchpoints

QR codes in physical locations (posters, receipts, product packaging) add an entirely new click channel. Every QR scan is tracked just like a link click, so you can measure offline-to-online conversion rates.

10. Track, Learn, and Iterate

The most important tip: continuously review your link analytics. Look at which links get the most clicks, which channels perform best, and what time windows drive peak engagement. Use these insights to refine your approach with every campaign.

Summary
