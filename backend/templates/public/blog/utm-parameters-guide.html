UTM parameters are the backbone of campaign tracking. If you're running any kind of digital marketing email, social media, paid ads UTM tags are how you connect clicks to campaigns in your analytics platform. This guide covers everything you need to know.

What Are UTM Parameters?

UTM (Urchin Tracking Module) parameters are tags added to the end of a URL. When someone clicks a link with UTM parameters, those tags are sent to your analytics tool (like Google Analytics), so you can see exactly where your traffic comes from.

A URL with UTM parameters looks like this:

https://example.com/page?utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=spring_sale

The 5 UTM Parameters Explained

1. utm_source (Required)

Identifies which platform or site sent the traffic.

Examples: google, facebook, x, newsletter, linkedin

2. utm_medium (Required)

Identifies the marketing channel or type of traffic.

Examples: cpc, email, social, organic, referral, banner

3. utm_campaign (Required)

Identifies the specific campaign, promotion, or offer.

Examples: spring_sale, product_launch, black_friday_2025

4. utm_term (Optional)

Identifies paid search keywords. Useful for PPC campaigns.

Examples: url+shortener, link+management, qr+code+generator

5. utm_content (Optional)

Differentiates similar content or links within the same campaign. Great for A/B testing.

Examples: header_cta, sidebar_banner, footer_link, blue_button

UTM Best Practices

Use lowercase consistently

UTM parameters are case-sensitive. "Facebook" and "facebook" will show as separate sources. Stick to lowercase.

Use underscores instead of spaces

Spaces get encoded as %20 in URLs, making them hard to read. Use underscores or hyphens: spring_sale not spring sale.

Create a naming convention document

Document your UTM naming rules and share with your team. Consistency is the key to clean analytics data.

Shorten UTM-tagged URLs

Long UTM URLs look messy in social posts and emails. Use a URL shortener like TinlyLink to create clean, trackable short links.

Don't use UTMs for internal links

Only tag links for external traffic. Using UTMs on internal site links will overwrite the original source data in your analytics.

Real-World UTM Examples

Email newsletter

?utm_source=newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_digest&utm_content=top_story

Facebook ad

?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=product_launch&utm_content=video_ad

Twitter bio link

?utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=profile_link
