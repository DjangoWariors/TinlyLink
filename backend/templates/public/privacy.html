Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 1, 2025

At TinlyLink, we take your privacy seriously. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and what choices you have. It applies to all users of our website, dashboard, API, and related services (collectively, the "Service").

By using TinlyLink, you agree to the collection and use of information as described in this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account Information

When you create an account, we collect:

  • Email address — used for login, notifications, and account recovery.
  • Name — displayed in your profile and team workspaces.
  • Password — stored as a one-way hash (Argon2). We never store or see your plain-text password.

If you sign in with Google OAuth, we receive your name and email from Google. We do not access your Google contacts, calendar, or other account data.

1.2 Link and QR Code Data

When you use the Service, we store:

  • Original (destination) URLs you shorten.
  • Short link slugs, tags, and metadata you assign.
  • QR code configuration (colors, style, logo, content).
  • Campaign and UTM parameters you attach to links.

1.3 Click Analytics Data

When someone clicks a shortened link or scans a QR code, we collect anonymized analytics data:

  • IP address — used to determine approximate geographic location (country and city level), then discarded. We do not store raw IP addresses long-term.
  • User agent — parsed to determine device type, operating system, and browser. The raw user agent string is not retained.
  • Referrer URL — the page that linked to your short URL.
  • Timestamp — when the click occurred.
  • UTM parameters — if present in the link, these are captured for campaign attribution.

This data is associated with your links, not with the individuals who click them. We do not build profiles of link visitors or track them across different links.

1.4 Payment Information

If you subscribe to a paid plan, payment is processed by Stripe. We do not store your full credit card number. We receive and store only:

  • Last four digits of your card (for display in your billing settings).
  • Card brand and expiration date.
  • Stripe customer and subscription identifiers.
  • Invoice and payment history.

1.5 Technical and Usage Data

We automatically collect certain information when you use the dashboard:

  • Browser type and version.
  • Device type and operating system.
  • Pages visited within the Service.
  • Session duration and interaction patterns.

This helps us understand how the Service is used and identify areas for improvement.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide the Service. Shorten links, generate QR codes, redirect clicks, and display analytics in your dashboard.
  • Manage your account. Authenticate you, process billing, and communicate account-related updates (password resets, billing receipts, plan changes).
  • Improve the Service. Analyze usage patterns to fix bugs, improve performance, and develop new features.
  • Prevent abuse. Detect and block spam, phishing, malware distribution, and other violations of our Terms of Service.
  • Provide support. Respond to your questions and troubleshoot issues.

We do not sell your personal information. We do not use your data for targeted advertising.

3. Information Sharing

We share your information only in the following circumstances:

3.1 Service Providers

We use third-party services to operate TinlyLink. These providers have access to your data only as needed to perform their functions:

  • Stripe — payment processing.
  • SendGrid — transactional emails (account verification, password resets, billing receipts).
  • Amazon Web Services (AWS) — cloud infrastructure and file storage.
  • Sentry — error monitoring and crash reporting (no personal data is sent).
  • MaxMind GeoIP — IP-to-location lookup for click analytics (IP addresses are not shared; lookups happen on our servers).

3.2 Team Members

If you use team workspaces, other members of your team can see links, QR codes, and analytics data that belong to the team. Your personal links and data remain private unless you explicitly share them.

3.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required by law, court order, or government request. We will attempt to notify you before doing so, unless legally prohibited.

3.4 Business Transfers

If TinlyLink is acquired or merged with another company, your data may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any such change and any choices you may have.

4. Data Retention

We retain your data as follows:

Data Type Retention Period
Account information Until you delete your account
Links and QR codes Until you delete them or close your account
Click analytics (Free plan) 30 days
Click analytics (Pro plan) 1 year
Click analytics (Business plan) Indefinitely
Payment records As required by tax and financial regulations
Server logs 30 days

When you delete your account, we remove your personal data within 30 days. Some anonymized, aggregated data (such as total click counts) may be retained for service analytics.

5. Cookies

TinlyLink uses a minimal number of cookies:

  • Session cookie — keeps you logged in. Expires after 7 days of inactivity. Essential for the Service to function.
  • CSRF token — protects against cross-site request forgery. Essential for security.

We do not use advertising cookies, tracking pixels, or third-party analytics cookies. We do not participate in cross-site tracking or ad networks.

Note: The interstitial page shown for free-plan links may display advertisements served by Google AdSense. Google may set its own cookies for ad personalization. You can manage Google's ad settings at adssettings.google.com.

6. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption in transit. All connections use HTTPS/TLS. Data is encrypted between your browser and our servers.
  • Password hashing. Passwords are hashed with Argon2, a memory-hard algorithm designed to resist brute-force attacks.
  • Access controls. Internal access to user data is restricted to essential personnel and protected by multi-factor authentication.
  • Infrastructure security. Our servers run on hardened, regularly updated infrastructure with automated security patching.
  • Token security. API keys and JWT tokens are signed and have limited lifetimes. Refresh tokens are rotated and blacklisted after use.

No system is perfectly secure. If we discover a data breach that affects your personal information, we will notify you promptly and take steps to mitigate the impact.

7. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access. You can view all the data we hold about you through your dashboard and account settings.
  • Export. You can export your links, QR codes, and analytics data from your account settings at any time.
  • Correction. You can update your account information directly from your profile settings.
  • Deletion. You can delete individual links and QR codes, or close your entire account to remove all data.
  • Restriction. You can request that we limit processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Portability. You can request your data in a machine-readable format (JSON or CSV).

To exercise any of these rights, use the tools in your account settings or contact us at privacy@tinlylink.com. We respond to all requests within 30 days.

8. International Data Transfers

TinlyLink's servers are located in the United States. If you are accessing the Service from outside the US, your data will be transferred to and processed in the US.

We take appropriate safeguards to ensure your data is protected in accordance with this Privacy Policy, regardless of where it is processed.

9. Children's Privacy

TinlyLink is not intended for children under 16 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that a child under 16 has created an account, we will promptly delete it and all associated data.

10. Third-Party Links

TinlyLink is a link management platform — by its nature, it redirects users to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of destination websites. Each shortened link leads to a URL chosen by the link creator, not by TinlyLink.

We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any website you visit through a shortened link.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will notify you by email or by posting a notice on the Service. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page indicates when the most recent changes were published.

Continued use of the Service after changes take effect constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.

12. Contact Us

If you have questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or how we handle your data, contact us at:

  • Email: privacy@tinlylink.com

We aim to respond to all privacy-related inquiries within 30 days.
